Option A Department-level Col3

Col3 shows curated content for the whole department — top brands, featured products, a promo slot. It populates the moment the panel opens and never changes based on L2 hover. Subcategory items navigate on click.

StrengthsNo mouse trap. Col3 always populated. Simpler interaction model. Brand/product visibility at department level is useful for trade users who already know their brand.
Trade-offsCol3 is not subcategory-specific — "Popular in Plumbing" vs "Popular in Water Heaters." Less drill-down utility. Filter chips at department level are broader.

Option B Click to lock subcategory

Hovering L2 only highlights the item — Col3 doesn't react. Clicking an L2 subcategory "locks" it: the panel stays open, Col3 loads content specific to that subcategory. A second click (or "View All" in Col3) navigates. Below shows "Water Heaters" locked.

StrengthsCol3 is subcategory-specific and stable — no mouse trap. User can freely explore Col3 after clicking. Richer content at the right context level.
Trade-offsTwo-click model (click to preview, click to navigate) adds friction and is non-obvious. Needs clear visual differentiation between "locked" and "hover" states. Harder to communicate in stakeholder reviews.

Option C Progressive panels

Two-step reveal. State 1: hovering a department opens a focused panel showing L2 subcategories only — no Col3, no distractions. State 2: clicking an L2 subcategory replaces the panel with L3 items on the left + the full content column on the right. A breadcrumb back button returns to L2.

StrengthsZero ambiguity. Every click has one clear outcome. Col3 content is always anchored to a committed choice, so it can be fully subcategory-specific. No mouse trap by design. Works identically well on mobile.
Trade-offsMore clicks to reach L3. "See the whole catalog" overview is lost — you can only see one branch at a time. Significant departure from Concept 7 spec, needs stakeholder alignment. No brand/product visibility until L2 is clicked.
